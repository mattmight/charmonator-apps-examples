About the Undiagnosed Diseases Network

The UDN is a research study funded by the National Institutes of Health (NIH) that brings together clinical and research experts from across the United States to solve the most challenging medical mysteries.

Typical UDN candidates have:

  • A condition that has remained undiagnosed despite extensive medical evaluation
  • Symptoms that are chronic, complex, or progressive
  • A medical journey spanning multiple specialists and institutions
  • Prior genetic testing that was inconclusive or negative
  • A suspected rare or novel genetic condition
